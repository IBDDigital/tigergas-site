What is a CP12?

A CP12 is a Gas Safety Certificate. It is a legal requirement for landlords to have an annual gas safety check carried out on all gas appliances in a rental property. The CP12 certificate is issued by a Gas Safe registered engineer after a successful inspection.

Failure Categories

If an appliance fails a gas safety check, it will be classified into one of the following categories:

  • ID (Immediately Dangerous) - The appliance poses an immediate risk to life and must be disconnected straight away.
  • AR (At Risk) - The appliance is not immediately dangerous but could become so. It should be repaired or replaced as soon as possible.
  • NCS (Not Current Standards) - The appliance does not meet current installation standards but is not necessarily dangerous. It should be monitored and updated when possible.
